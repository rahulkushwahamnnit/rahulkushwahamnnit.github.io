Event Format

This event will be of 10 days.

It will start on 7th October 2014!

Difficulty level of questions will increase daily.

Rules and Regulations

1.This is an individual event and runs for 10 days.

2.There will be total 10 questions (3 - easy, 4 - medium, 3 - hard).

3.One question will show up every day at 10 p.m. and will be open for the next 24 hours.

4.The questions will be of increasing difficulty.

5.Users are free to submit as many times as they want but remember only the last submission will contribute to the scoring. All the other submissions except the last one are skipped over and only the last one is judged at the end of the day. There is no additional penalty for incorrect submissions.

6.Scoring: Multipliers for each problem will be based on its difficulty level.

7.Base points for each problem will initially be 100.

8.The value of the base points you get for the day depends on the number of penalty counts (PC) you receive for the day. Note that PC is a global entity and applies uniformly to all the users.

Rules and Regulations

• The PC initially (when the problem shows up at 10pm) is zero.

1.First correct submission will get all base points. The PC is now set to one.

2.With every passing hour from the time of first correct submission, PC is incremented by one.

3.The minimum number of base points you receive for the day is 30.

4.The number of base points you receive for the day is (100 - 4 * PC) or 30 whichever is greater.

• The final score for an individual on a particular day will be calculated as: Base Points for the Day * Multiplier for that level.

• Ties will be broken down firstly by the number of total submissions you make from the start of the contest and secondly by the sum of time at which the user has submitted the accepted solution.

Results of each day’s question will be declared by midnight on the next day.

• Overall winner will be declared after completion of the event.

• Use of any unfair means will be dealt with immediate disqualification and judge's decision will be treated as final. No correspondence in this regard will be entertained.

Ans. Correct solution means

1. It should give the correct output in the specified format. 2. Any presentation error will lead to a wrong answer.
