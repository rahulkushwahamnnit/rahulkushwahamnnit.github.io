Event Format

Round 1:

Abstract submission - Participants would mail a brief overview of their idea or work in about 300 words detailing the scope of their work, the basic principles involved, analytical treatment of the matter and results. The papers must be sent in either .doc or .pdf file. Try to be specific in details.

Round 2:

Paper submission - Selected participants must prepare a power point presentation of at most 15 slides incorporating the field of the application, background of the innovation, circuit or model of the application design, analytical and mathematical treatment, Cost structure, feasibility estimation, simulations and results along with scope for improvements. Presentation must be of maximum 15 minutes Selected students have to submit an IEEE paper in Double Column Standard Format which must cover all above discussed topics in either .doc or .pdf file. ther .doc or .pdf file.

Round 3:

Selected participants would be required to give a Power point presentation, detailing their work, innovation, findings and results, before an esteemed panel of judges, in about 15+5 minutes. The presentation would be followed by questions from the panel of judges and the auditions. Final teams have to submit IEEE paper to judges.

Important Points

  • Paper must be in IEEE two column format.
  • Intimation about the selection of paper will be given through mail latest.
  • Selected participants have to bring their power point presentations in a pen drive and in email address attachment.
  • For each participant 15 minutes time will be given for presentation and 5 minutes for question answer.
  • All participants must bring their college ID cards at the time of the event
  • Decisions of judges in final round and coordinators in initial stages shall be finals and it will be binding to all. The judges could comprise of personnel from college professors associated to IEEE.

Rules and Regulations

  • All the participants have to register themselves online.
  • Teams can have a maximum of 4 participants and minimum of 2 participants.
  • At least one member from all participating teams must be a student of Electrical or Electronics engineering and applied disciplines.
  • Deadlines of every round must be followed strictly
  • Participants could be from any year and any institute. Participants from different institutes may form a team provided their college administration allows them.
  • For Paper Presentation, the topic of the paper should be based on emerging / advance technology and recent developments in the respective branch of Engineering.
  • You are free to submit any number of abstracts but only one would be considered for presentation and the selection lies with the organizing team.
  • Author and co-author must be student of technical institute.
  • One paper will be presented by only one author.
  • Certificate will be issued to participant, who has registered the event and presented the paper.
  • Prizes and certificates will be given to the best presentation based on the criteria decided by the judges.
